For recruiters

Using the assessment to hire a supply chain manager

A short guide to running the assessment fairly and getting real signal from it.

How it works

The candidate answers 28 questions across the seven competencies. Each answer carries a score from 0 to 3 — situational questions give partial credit for sensible-but-weaker choices, so the result reflects judgement rather than guesswork. The output is a competency profile: an overall score, a bar for each of the seven areas, the candidate's strengths, their development areas, and a set of interview questions tailored to those weaker areas.

Reading the score bands

BandScoreInterpretation
Expert85%+Strong, well-grounded judgement across the board.
Advanced70–84%Solid; ready for the role with normal onboarding.
Proficient50–69%Capable but with clear gaps worth probing in interview.
Developingunder 50%Notable gaps; suitable for more junior roles or with development.

Running it well

  • Administer it consistently — same instructions and conditions for every candidate. This is what makes results comparable and fair.
  • Use the per-competency profile, not just the headline number. A candidate strong on negotiation but weak on due diligence is a different hire from the reverse — match the profile to what your role actually needs most.
  • Use the suggested interview probes to turn weak areas into a structured conversation rather than a rejection.
  • Print or save the report (PDF) to attach to the candidate record.
Fairness & good practice. Treat this as one input among several — interviews, references and a practical task. Don't use a single cut-off score as an automatic reject. Make reasonable adjustments for candidates who need them, and keep the same process for everyone so your hiring is consistent and defensible.

What it does not do

It doesn't measure personality, culture fit, or domain specifics unique to your industry (cold-chain, defence, pharma, etc.). Pair it with role-specific questions of your own. It also stores nothing — results live only in the browser until you print them — so there's no candidate data to manage on a server.
